John Amos Comenius was a Czech philosopher, pedagogue and theologian from the Margraviate of Moravia and is considered the father of modern education. He served as the last bishop of Unity of the Brethren and became a religious refugee and one of the earliest champions of universal education, a concept eventually set forth in his book Didactica Magna. As an educator and theologian, he led schools and advised governments across Protestant Europe through the middle of the seventeenth century.

Comenius was the innovator who first introduced pictorial textbooks, written in native language instead of Latin, applied effective teaching based on the natural gradual growth from simple to more comprehensive concepts, supported lifelong learning and development of logical thinking by moving from dull memorization, presented and supported the idea of equal opportunity for impoverished children, opened doors to education for women, and made instruction universal and practical.

Main dates in biography of Jan Komensky:

  • 28 March 1592: Komensky was born in Margraviate of Moravia in the Bohemian Crown in Uherský Brod.
  • 1604: his parents and two of his four sisters died in 1604 and young John went to live with his aunt in Strážnice.
  • 1614: entered the Latin school in Přerov, where he later returned as a teacher.
  • 1616: became rector of a school in Přerov, was ordained into the ministry of the Moravian Brethren and soon became pastor and rector at Fulnek, one of its most flourishing churches.
  • 1621: as a consequence of the religious wars, Komensky lost all his property and his writings.
  • 1627: led the Brethren into exile when the Habsburg Counter-Reformation persecuted the Protestants in Bohemia.
  • 1631:outlined a system of schools that is the exact counterpart of the existing English system of kindergarten or pre-school, elementary school, secondary school, college, and university.
  • 1638: traveled to Sweden to draw up a scheme for the management of the schools of the country.
  • 1639: published his 'Pansophiæ Prodromus', the series of textbooks, where he attempts to organize the entire field of human knowledge so as to bring it, in outline, within the grasp of every child.
  • 1641: upon request of the English Parliament, Kamensky joined a commission there charged with the reform of the system of public education.
  • 1642: returned to Sweden to work on the task of reorganizing the Swedish schools.
  • 1650: Kamensky was invited to Sarospatak as a professor at the first Hungarian Protestant College; he wrote some of his most important works there.
  • 1658: the 'Orbis Pictus' was published, probably the most renowned and most widely circulated of school textbooks.
  • 15 November 1670: died aged 78 in Amsterdam, Dutch Republic.

Jan Komensky
